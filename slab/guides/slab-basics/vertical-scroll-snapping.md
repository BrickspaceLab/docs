# Vertical scroll snapping

Vertical scroll snapping lets sections lock into place as customers scroll through the storefront. Use it when you want a more guided, section-by-section browsing experience.

## How it works

In Slab, vertical scroll snapping has two parts.

First, enable **Enable scroll snapping** in the global **Layout** settings. This turns the main page area into a vertical snap container.

Then open the sections you want to snap and choose a **Scroll snap alignment** value. If a section is left on **None**, it will keep scrolling normally.

Slab also disables snapping automatically for customers who prefer reduced motion.

## Enable scroll snapping globally

1. Open the theme editor.
2. Go to **Theme settings**.
3. Open **Layout**.
4. Enable **Enable scroll snapping**.

Once this is enabled, supported sections will show the **Scroll snap alignment** setting.

## Set scroll snap alignment on sections

1. Go back to the section you want to control.
2. Find **Scroll snap alignment**.
3. Choose the alignment you want for that section.

You can repeat this for each section that should participate in scroll snapping.

## Scroll snap alignment options

### Start

Use **Start** to snap the section to the top of the viewport.

### Center

Use **Center** to snap the section to the middle of the viewport.

### End

Use **End** to snap the section to the bottom of the viewport.

### None

Use **None** when you want that section to scroll normally without snapping.

## Important note

Turning on **Enable scroll snapping** does not force every section to snap automatically. Each section still needs its own **Scroll snap alignment** setting selected before snapping will happen.
